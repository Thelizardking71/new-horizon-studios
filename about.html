About the Artist

Hi, I’m Dan. I used to be a Director of Finance and IT until my heart decided it was done with spreadsheets and tried to kill me in late 2025. Three strokes and a bunch of medical bills later, I’m now the proud owner of a dodgy ticker, partial blindness, and a government-mandated second career as a professional photographer.

I live in Cronulla and spend my days pointing a camera at the same coastline that’s been trying to drown me since I was a kid. I shoot the cliffs, the rock pools, the moody skies, and the occasional seagull giving me the side-eye. Basically, if it looks like it wants to fight the ocean and lose, I photograph it.

When I’m not behind the lens, I’m usually arguing with my ex about rent, trying to convince my teenagers that I’m still cool, or explaining to Centrelink that “retired due to heart failure” is not the same as “on a permanent holiday.”

I started this whole thing because staring at the ceiling and feeling sorry for myself got boring. So now I sell prints of the places that nearly killed me, in the hope that someone else might enjoy them from the safety of their living room.

If you buy something, you’re not just getting a photo — you’re helping keep a stubborn, half-blind, slightly broken photographer in coffee and camera gear. And for that, I genuinely thank you.

Welcome to New Horizon Studios.
It’s moody, it’s coastal, and like me, it’s still standing… barely.
